February 2026

A Season Paused, Not a Dream Ended

On February 8, 2026, Iowa State University announced the cancellation of the remainder of its women's gymnastics season. The decision came down to one thing: the health and safety of the student-athletes who represent the Cyclones.

"The health, safety and overall welfare of our student-athletes is paramount, and it is our responsibility to ensure that they are able to safely train and compete at the highest level."

‚Äì Shamaree Brown, Senior Associate AD for Student Services, Iowa State University

With five meets remaining before the Big 12 Championships, Iowa State made the call to cancel the remainder of the season. Our hearts go out to the student-athletes who dedicated themselves to this program and who deserve every opportunity to compete at the highest level.

But here's what this is not: it's not the end of Cyclone gymnastics. It's a moment that demands our collective support, our advocacy, and our commitment to the future of this program. Once a Cyclone, Always a Cyclone.

Why This Matters

The Premier Women's Collegiate Sport Needs Every Program

Women's collegiate gymnastics is the female equivalent of football on the male side. The pageantry is phenomenal. The energy in a packed arena is electric. The fan experience is unlike anything else in women's athletics. And with Olympic women's gymnastics as the number one most-watched sport globally during the Games, the upside for the collegiate game is enormous.

But this sport doesn't reach its full potential when programs struggle. A program that succeeds and then lifts others creates momentum that benefits everyone. That's not just a talking point. It's the foundation of everything the Women's Collegiate Gymnastics Association stands for.

Right now, it's our turn to rally around the Cyclones, their student-athletes, and everyone who has given everything to this program. We support them. We fight for their future. And we make sure they come back stronger, better resourced, and ready to compete at the highest level.

This Sport Transforms Lives.
Every Program Matters.

Our gymnasts graduate at a 99% rate, lead all women's NCAA sports in GPA, and 76.8% are Scholastic All-Americans. They lead student organizations, volunteer in communities, and graduate ready to make an immediate impact. As Olympic gold medalist Bart Conner says: "They're the epitome of what a top-level student-athlete should be. It's the best money the university can spend."

The Path Forward

Iowa State Will Come Back Bigger and Better

This is a temporary chapter in a long and proud Cyclone gymnastics story. Programs across the country have faced difficult moments and emerged stronger because the people around them believed in the mission and invested in the future.

The Women's Collegiate Gymnastics Association and the Collegiate Gymnastics Growth Initiative stand firmly alongside Iowa State's administration, coaching staff, and student-athletes. We believe in this program. We believe in this sport. And we believe that when Iowa State returns to the competition floor, the Cyclones and the Hilton Coliseum crowd will remind everyone why women's collegiate gymnastics is the most electrifying experience in women's athletics.

The world is watching. The opportunity is here. The time is now.
